## UPI Payment Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, targeting users who rely on digital payments. In a typical case,
a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer.
The scam usually begins with the fraudster gaining the victim’s trust. They may claim that money needs to be received or
refunded and send a “collect request” on a UPI app. The victim is then instructed to enter their UPI PIN to receive money,
which is misleading because entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment.
In some cases, fake links or QR codes are shared. When scanned, these can either request money or redirect the user to a fake website
that captures sensitive information.
Students and young users are often targeted due to lack of awareness. The consequences include financial loss, unauthorized transactions,
and sometimes complete draining of bank accounts.
This type of fraud highlights the importance of understanding how UPI systems work and being cautious while handling unknown payment requests.
